4. Investment Risks

ALL INVESTMENTS INVOLVE RISK. You should be aware of and willing to accept the following risks:

4.1 Market Risk

The value of investments can decrease due to factors affecting the overall economy, stock markets, bond markets, or specific industries. Market downturns can occur unexpectedly and may last for extended periods.

4.2 Loss of Principal

You may lose some or all of your invested capital. Unlike bank deposits, securities are not insured by the FDIC, SIPC, or any other government agency against loss.

4.3 Inflation Risk

Returns may not keep pace with inflation, reducing your purchasing power over time.

4.4 Interest Rate Risk

Changes in interest rates can affect the value of bonds and other fixed-income investments.

4.5 Liquidity Risk

Some investments may be difficult to sell quickly at a fair price, especially in illiquid markets.

4.6 Currency Risk

Investments in foreign securities expose you to exchange rate fluctuations that can affect returns.

4.7 Concentration Risk

Putting too much money into a single investment or sector increases your vulnerability to losses in that area.

4.8 Leveraged and Derivative Products

Trading with borrowed money, options, futures, and other derivatives can amplify both gains and losses significantly.

5. Past Performance Does Not Predict Future Results

  • "Expert" predictions about markets frequently prove incorrect

6. Do Your Own Research (DYOR)

Before making any investment decision, you should:

  • Thoroughly research the investment opportunity independently
  • Read official documents like prospectuses, annual reports, and SEC filings
  • Understand the business model, competition, and risks
  • Consider consulting multiple sources of information
  • Evaluate whether the investment aligns with your goals and risk tolerance

Never make investment decisions based solely on information from a single source, including InvestWise.

7. Consult Qualified Professionals

We strongly recommend that you consult with qualified professionals before making investment decisions:

  • Registered Investment Advisors (RIA) - Provide personalized investment advice
  • Certified Financial Planners (CFP) - Help with comprehensive financial planning
  • Certified Public Accountants (CPA) - Address tax implications
  • Estate Planning Attorneys - Help with wealth transfer strategies
  • Full-service broker-dealers - Can provide research and execution services

These professionals can evaluate your individual financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance to provide appropriate recommendations.
